Overview

Purpose

When life gets in the way we tend to put necessary tasks on the backburner. As the due date comes the task must be completed quickly. Oftentimes the consequences are dire, and there isn’t much space for error. We’ve all experienced this at one time or another, and if you’re anything like me it’s chronic. That’s why I’ve created the United Procrastinators Association. The UPA’s website is to help educate fellow procrastinators on how they can put off their to-do-list in the most efficient way possible. Are you a procrastinator? Do you find yourself failing to finish strong? Tips to help are only one click away!

Audience

This website is made for anyone who has a habit of procrastinating and needs help getting assignments done on time and productively. If you have a desire to put your best work out in a short amount of time then the United Procrastinators Association is here to help you. Anyone is welcome and all are encouraged to try out our tips for their next project reguardless of whether you are in high school, college, or in your profession.

Home page

There are many instances of where people have been able to complete their procrastinated work before the assigned deadline thanks to the insights and tips from the United Procrastinators Association. Due to the calculated formula that we have created these people are able to live their best lives as well as accomplish other important tasks. As the wise Edward J. Stieglitz once said “... in the end it's not the years in your life that count; it's the life in your years”. We only have a limited amount of time here on Earth, and what matters most is that we use it wisely. We pride ourselves in being able to enable people to make the most of that time that they have. The application of our tips and tricks will allow anyone to save more time for the more important and enjoyable things in life. Whether you value time spent with family, putting more time in at the gym, relaxing at the pool, or anything else we can help you relish more of it. Page 1

Procrastination can look like a variety of things. It can be as simple as taking a quick nap or as extravagant as going on a vacation or to a party. There is not anything inherently wrong with these activities on their own, but when it comes to meeting deadlines it’s important that they do not stop you from getting tasks done. In many cases these activities add to your ability to function at your best level, and therefore, it can be used to finish assignments in a short amount of time. This section of the United Procrastinators Association website will give you helpful tips on how you can make procrastination work in your favor.
